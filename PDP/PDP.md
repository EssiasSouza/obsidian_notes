# PDP - Essias document

This document represents my long-term Professional Development Plan (PDP) focused on becoming a strong international Cloud Native / Platform Engineering professional by February 2028.

The strategy is intentionally divided into three phases of seven months each. This structure allows continuous improvement, practical consolidation and periodic reassessment through PDCA cycles instead of following a rigid and static roadmap.

The main goal is not to learn every technology available in the cloud ecosystem, since that would be unrealistic and inefficient. The goal is to develop strong fundamentals, practical engineering skills, architectural thinking, communication ability and international-level technical maturity.

My current target for 2028 is to be fully prepared to compete for international opportunities, either internally through multinational environments such as Corpay or externally in global markets such as Spain, Finland, Canada, Netherlands, Germany and other mature technology ecosystems.

The complete roadmap will cover approximately 21 months, starting in June 2026 and ending in February 2028.

# Long-Term Strategy

## Phase 1

### June 2026 → December 2026

### Status: Active Plan

This is the current execution phase and the primary focus of the PDP.

The objective of Phase 1 is to build strong Cloud Native engineering foundations and reduce technical insecurity by consolidating practical experience and deep understanding of core concepts.

This phase will focus on six strategic pillars:

* Kubernetes
* AWS
* Linux and Networking
* Infrastructure as Code and Automation
* Observability and Security
* Technical English

The learning model will follow the 70-20-10 framework:

| Category      | Percentage | Purpose                                                                                   |
| ------------- | ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Experience    | 70%        | Hands-on labs, troubleshooting, implementation and real-world practice                    |
| Communication | 20%        | Documentation, technical discussions, architecture explanations and English communication |
| Learning      | 10%        | Courses, documentation, books and theoretical study                                       |

The intention is to prioritize practical engineering capability over excessive theoretical consumption.

At the end of this phase, a complete PDCA review will be performed in order to evaluate:

* technical evolution
* communication progress
* confidence level
* practical experience
* market readiness
* learning efficiency
* possible roadmap adjustments

The roadmap is intentionally flexible and adaptive.

The objective is not perfection.
The objective is sustainable progression toward international readiness.

# Suggested Future Direction

## Phase 2

### January 2027 → July 2027

### Status: Direction Suggestion

The second phase is expected to focus on architectural maturity and platform engineering evolution.

Possible focus areas:

* advanced Kubernetes operations
* distributed systems
* platform engineering
* GitOps maturity
* observability strategy
* cloud governance
* reliability engineering
* system design
* technical leadership
* mentoring
* cloud security awareness

The expected outcome of this phase is the transition from operational cloud knowledge into broader engineering and architectural capability.

This phase may be adjusted according to the results of the Phase 1 PDCA review.

# Suggested Future Direction

## Phase 3

### August 2027 → February 2028

### Status: Direction Suggestion

The third phase is expected to focus on international market preparation and professional positioning.

Possible focus areas:

* advanced technical communication in English
* mock interviews
* international resume refinement
* architecture interviews
* behavioral interviews
* technical storytelling
* GitHub portfolio
* technical content creation
* networking with international communities
* cloud certifications if strategically necessary

The expected outcome is to become fully prepared for international opportunities with sufficient technical confidence, communication maturity and practical engineering experience.

# Final Principle

This PDP is based on the understanding that modern cloud engineering is too broad to master completely.

The objective is not to know every tool.

The objective is to become:

* technically reliable
* architecturally aware
* operationally effective
* security conscious
* communication capable
* adaptable to different enterprise environments

The long-term focus is sustainable growth, practical experience and international professional readiness.
