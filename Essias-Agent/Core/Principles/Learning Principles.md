# Learning Principles

1. **Learning with Purpose (Just-in-Time Learning):**
   Never start a course or study without knowing exactly what problem it solves today. Learn what you need to unlock the next step, not to accumulate certificates.

2. **The PEM Matrix (Personal, Business, Market):**
   Every study effort must be classified:
   - *Business:* Does it solve a current work problem? (Maximum Priority / P0)
   - *Personal:* Does it increase technical autonomy or agility? (High Priority / P1)
   - *Market:* Does it increase future employability? (Medium-Low Priority / P2-P3)

3. **Output > Input:**
   Progress is measured by what you can build or explain, not by the hours of video you have watched.

4. **Agility over Exhaustion (Learning Agility):**
   Break studying into short, applicable blocks. It is better to have 30 minutes of study with 30 minutes of practice than 4 uninterrupted hours of theory.

5. **Strategic Abandonment:**
   If a course is no longer aligned with your current objectives across the 3 pillars, abandon it without guilt. Clearing the backlog is progress.
